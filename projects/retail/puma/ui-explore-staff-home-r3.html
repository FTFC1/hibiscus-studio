The 5-30-60 Rule
REVIEW
The rule: Eye contact within 5 seconds. Greet within 30. Approach within 60.
Watch for: Customers who linger near a display for more than 20 seconds — they want help but won't ask.
"What brings you in today?" "Looking for anything specific?"
Kill "Can I Help You?"
REVIEW
Why it fails: Closed question. Customer says "just looking" reflexively. Conversation dead.
Instead: Comment on what they're looking at. Ask about their day. Be a person first.
"Those just came in yesterday" "That's our best seller this month"
Customer picks up a shoe, looks at the price, puts it down. Don't say "Can I help?" — say "That one runs really well for the price. What are you using it for?"
The 70/30 Rule
NOW
The rule: Customer talks 70%. You talk 30%. Guide with questions, not pitches.
Watch for: When you catch yourself explaining for more than 15 seconds — stop. Ask a question.
Questions that work:
"What do you use them for mostly?" "What matters more — comfort or looks?" "Have you tried this brand before?"
Customer: "I need running shoes." Don't launch into features. Say: "Nice — are you training for something specific, or just general fitness?" Let them tell you what they need. Then match.
