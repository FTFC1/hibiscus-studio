# What Goes in Telegram vs. What Goes in Selar
**Platform Roles: Learning vs. Engagement**

---

## Quick Answer

**Selar = Structured learning** (missions, quizzes, certificates)
**Telegram = Daily engagement** (tips, wins, community)

**Think:** Selar is the classroom, Telegram is the break room.

---

## SELAR (Learning Platform)

### What's in Selar:

**24 Missions (800-1,200 words each):**
- Mission 1: The 5-30-60 Rule
- Mission 2: Kill "Can I Help You?"
- Mission 3: The 70/30 Rule
- Mission 4-6: Objection handling
- Mission 7-12: Cross-selling systems
- Mission 13-18: Product knowledge
- Mission 19-24: PUMA Playbook

**Each mission includes:**
- Full lesson text (mobile-optimized, 8-10 min read)
- Real examples from Nigerian retail context
- Step-by-step scripts staff can use
- Quick Reference card (bookmark-able cheat sheet)
- 5-question quiz (80% to pass, auto-graded)
- Practical task instructions ("Practice this technique today")

**Drip schedule:**
- Missions unlock Mon/Wed/Fri automatically
- Can't skip ahead (must pass quiz to unlock next)
- Staff access on phones anytime (no app needed, browser-based)

**Manager dashboard:**
- See all staff progress (completed, in-progress, not started)
- View quiz scores
- Export completion data to CSV

**Certificates:**
- Auto-generated when staff complete Module 1-4
- Downloadable PDF

### What's NOT in Selar:
- Daily tips/reminders (that's Telegram)
- Peer conversations (that's Telegram)
- Manager announcements (that's Telegram)
- Real-time Q&A (that's Telegram)

## Why Both Platforms?

**"Why not just Telegram?"**
- No quiz validation (honor system doesn't prove learning)
- No drip content (staff could race through 24 missions in one day)
- No completion tracking (can't see who's done what)
- No certificates (no formal credential)
- Too much scrolling (finding Mission 8 in chat history = nightmare)

**"Why not just Selar?"**
- No daily engagement (staff log in Mon/Wed/Fri only, forget between)
- No peer community (isolated learning, no wins shared)
- No real-time Q&A (Selar is async, questions sit unanswered)
- No manager nudges (no way to say "Mission unlocks in 1 hour!")

**Together:** Selar provides structure, Telegram provides motivation.

- **Micro-tips** (1-3 sentences, quick reminders)

### Rule of Thumb:

- **If it needs to be graded:** Selar
- **If it's peer-to-peer:** Telegram

## Technical Details

**How does Telegram link to Selar?**
- Manager posts Selar course link in Telegram pinned message
- Staff tap link â†’ Opens browser â†’ Logs into Selar â†’ Sees mission
- One-time login (Selar remembers device)

**Can staff comment on missions in Selar?**
- No (Selar is one-way delivery)
- Questions/discussions happen in Telegram

**How do managers post to Telegram?**
- Manual posts (manager types message daily)
- OR: Pre-scheduled posts (Telegram has built-in scheduler)
- OR: Automation (Zapier/IFTTT can auto-post at set times, but requires setup)

**Recommendation for pilot:** Manual posts (builds authentic manager engagement). Automate later if scaling to 50+ staff.

---

## Bottom Line

**Selar = What they learn** (missions, quizzes, structure)
**Telegram = How they stay engaged** (tips, wins, community)

**Without Selar:** No proof of learning, no quiz validation, no drip control
**Without Telegram:** Staff forget between missions, no peer support, low motivation

**Together:** 80%+ completion rate, peer learning culture, daily habit formation.
