## üß† WHY CUSTOMERS LEAVE WITHOUT BUYING (AND WHY THEY'LL BUY LATER)

### Reason #1: Timing
They like it, but they're not ready to buy today. Maybe they:
- Just got paid and need to wait until next payday
- Want to check with someone first
- Are comparing options across stores
- Need time to think (legitimately)

**Follow-up opportunity:** Text them in 3-5 days: "Hey [name], just checking in‚Äîstill interested in those running shoes we looked at?"

---

### Reason #2: Budget
They love it, but it's outside their budget right now.

**Follow-up opportunity:** Notify them when it goes on sale or when similar items at lower price points arrive.

---

### Reason #3: Not the Perfect Match
They liked what you showed, but something was slightly off (color, fit, size out of stock).

**Follow-up opportunity:** Text them when the right color/size arrives: "Hi [name], those black track pants you wanted just came in!"

---

### Reason #4: Overwhelmed
Too many choices. Decision fatigue. They need space.

**Follow-up opportunity:** Send a narrowed-down recommendation: "Hey [name], thought about our conversation‚Äîthis one is probably your best bet."

## ‚úÖ WHAT TO DO INSTEAD: THE EXIT PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Acknowledge Their Decision (No Pressure)

**Don't say:** "Are you sure you don't want it?" ‚ùå
(Sounds desperate, creates awkwardness)

**Don't say:** Nothing (just let them leave) ‚ùå
(Missed opportunity‚Äîthey might buy later)

**Instead, say:** "No problem! Before you go‚Äîcan I grab your name and number? If we get new stock or if this goes on sale, I'll let you know."

**Why this works:**
- No pressure (they're leaving anyway)
- Clear benefit to them (you'll update them on sales/stock)
- Low commitment ask (just name + number, not full interrogation)

---

### Step 2: Capture Key Information

**Minimum information to capture:**
1. **Name** (first name is enough)
2. **Phone number** (WhatsApp is best in Nigeria)
3. **What they were looking for** (running shoes, hoodie, gym bag, etc.)

**Optional but helpful:**
4. **Size** (so you can notify when their size is back in stock)
5. **Budget range** (so you can suggest alternatives in their price range)
6. **Why they didn't buy today** (too expensive, wrong color, need to think, etc.)

**How to capture it:**

"Let me grab your info real quick‚Äîwhat's your name?"

[They tell you]

"And your number? I'll shoot you a text if this goes on sale."

[They give number]

"Perfect. And just so I know what to look out for‚Äîwhat size were you looking for?"

**Write it down immediately** (in your phone, notebook, or store CRM system).

---

### Step 3: Set Expectations for Follow-Up

**Don't say:** "I'll call you later." ‚ùå
(Vague, sounds like you'll pester them)

**Instead, say:** "I'll text you if [specific trigger]. If you don't hear from me in the next week, it means nothing came in‚Äîbut feel free to reach out anytime."

**Why this works:**
- Specific trigger (sale, new stock, right color arrives)
- Time-bound (1 week, not "someday")
- Gives them control ("feel free to reach out")

**Examples of specific triggers:**
- "I'll text you if these go on sale."
- "I'll let you know when the black version comes in."
- "I'll reach out if we get more size 42s."
- "I'll message you if something similar in your budget arrives."

---

### Step 4: The Soft Exit Line

After you've captured their info, end on a warm note:

"Sounds good! I'm [your name], by the way. Nice meeting you."

**Why this works:**
- Personal (they know your name now‚Äîless transactional)
- Friendly (warm goodbye, not "hope you buy something")
- Opens the door for future relationship (not one-off interaction)

## üöß COMMON MISTAKES

**Mistake 1: Asking for too much information**
"Can I get your name, number, email, Instagram, address, and what size you usually wear in every brand?"

**What you just did:** Turned a simple ask into an interrogation. They'll say no. Keep it minimal‚Äîname and number are enough.

---

**Mistake 2: Not explaining why you need their info**
"Can I get your number?"

**What they think:** "Why? Are you going to spam me?"

**What you should say:** "Can I get your number? I'll text you if this goes on sale."

**Now they understand the benefit.**

---

**Mistake 3: Promising vague follow-up**
"I'll let you know if anything happens."

**What they think:** "Yeah, sure you will."

**What you should say:** "I'll text you if these go on sale this month. If you don't hear from me by [date], nothing came up."

**Specific > vague.**

---

**Mistake 4: Not following up**
You captured their info‚Ä¶ and never texted them.

**What you just did:** Wasted the entire Exit Protocol. They gave you their number for a reason. Use it.

---

**Mistake 5: Following up too aggressively**
Day 1: "Hey, still interested?"
Day 2: "Just checking in!"
Day 3: "We have other options!"

**What you just did:** Became a pest. They'll block you.

**Follow up once within 3-7 days. If they don't respond, let it go.**

Even if they don't buy, you've captured their name and number when you tagged the hold item.

## üì± FOLLOW-UP BEST PRACTICES

### When to Follow Up:
- **3-5 days after visit** if no specific trigger (gentle check-in)
- **Immediately** if their trigger happens (sale starts, item restocked)
- **7-10 days** if they mentioned coming back but didn't (soft nudge)

### How to Follow Up (Text Templates):

**Sale notification:**
"Hi [name]! [Your name] from [store]. Those [item] you looked at are on sale this week‚Äî[discount]. Let me know if you want me to hold one!"

**Restock notification:**
"Hi [name], this is [your name] from [store]. Those [item] in [size/color] you wanted just came back in stock. Want me to set one aside?"

**General check-in:**
"Hey [name], [your name] from [store]. Just checking in‚Äîstill looking for [item]? Let me know if I can help!"

**New arrival:**
"Hi [name], [your name] from [store]. We just got in [similar item to what they wanted] in your price range. Want to see a pic?"

### When NOT to Follow Up:
- They explicitly said "don't contact me"
- They didn't respond to your first follow-up (let it go)
- It's been more than 2 weeks (lead is cold)
