# Mikano IPP Deal Pipeline Framework

## Executive Summary

This framework provides a comprehensive strategy for Mikano International to scale its IPP deal flow to 100 qualified opportunities per month. By leveraging Mikano's established track record with projects like the 27.2 MW JOTNA installation, the NNPC Abuja project, and the Intercontinental Hotel Lagos 4 MW CNG solution, we can systematically target, qualify, and convert high-potential clients across Nigeria.

## Qualification Criteria

### Technical Qualifiers

1. **Power Requirements**
   - Minimum demand: 500 kW continuous load
   - Optimal target: 1-10 MW demand
   - Load pattern: 70%+ capacity utilization

2. **Gas Access Feasibility**
   - Pipeline access within 10km
   - CNG/LNG delivery feasibility
   - Space for fuel storage if needed

3. **Site Suitability**
   - Minimum 200m² available space
   - Access for equipment installation
   - Proximity to main electrical distribution
   - Environmental compliance potential

### Commercial Qualifiers

1. **Financial Health**
   - Minimum 3 years of operation
   - Positive EBITDA for past 2 years
   - No major credit incidents
   - Ability to provide payment security

2. **Decision Process**
   - Clear ownership/authority structure
   - Reasonable approval timeline (<6 months)
   - Identifiable decision makers
   - Compatible procurement process

3. **Strategic Alignment**
   - Long-term operational outlook (5+ years)
   - Energy cost sensitivity
   - Reliability/quality requirements
   - Sustainability/environmental goals

## Addressing Nigerian IPP Market Challenges

### Regulatory Complexities

- **Challenge**: Multi-agency oversight and evolving regulatory framework
- **Solution**: Leverage Mikano's experience with NNPC and other projects to navigate permitting
- **Approach**: Dedicated regulatory affairs specialist to manage compliance process
- **Advantage**: Mikano's established relationships with regulatory bodies

### Gas Supply Reliability

- **Challenge**: Inconsistent gas delivery and pipeline vandalism
- **Solution**: Multi-fuel approach with backup systems (as used in JOTNA project)
- **Approach**: Custom fuel strategy for each client based on location and requirements
- **Advantage**: Mikano's experience with CNG alternatives (Intercontinental Hotel Lagos)

### Infrastructure Limitations

- **Challenge**: Weak transmission infrastructure and grid instability
- **Solution**: Appropriately sized, standalone power solutions with robust control systems
- **Approach**: Detailed site assessment to identify specific challenges
- **Advantage**: Mikano's turnkey capability from design to operation

### Financial Barriers

- **Challenge**: Limited client capital for energy infrastructure
- **Solution**: Zero-CAPEX, pure service model with payment security mechanisms
- **Approach**: Flexible contract structures with appropriate risk allocation
- **Advantage**: Mikano's financial strength and project finance experience
