# Head-Office Fashion-Retail Talent – Addressable Market Estimate (Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya)

**Objective** – size the pool of professionals eligible for the proposed Certificate (buyers, merchandisers, planners, VM, retail analysts). We triangulate three data lenses: national labour statistics, Euromonitor sector reports, and LinkedIn professional counts.

## 1. Formal Fashion-Retail Employment
| Country | 2023 Retail Employment* | Fashion-specific share (est. 8 %) | Head-office ratio (12 %) | Head-office head-count |

| **Total** | — | — | — | **≈65 400** |
*Sources: Euromonitor "Retailing in ... 2024" datasheets; NBS Q4 2023 labour force report.

## 2. LinkedIn Verification
Keyword search (May 2024):

Nigeria – 1 780 profiles  |  Ghana – 430 | Kenya – 590  
Observation: LinkedIn lightly captures ~5 % of high-skill retail staff, aligning with global ratios.

## 3. Blended TAM Assumption
Applying a *formal-sector focus* (40 % of table 1) and discounting overlaps yields **≈26 000** potential learners across the three markets today.

## 4. Growth Outlook (2024-2027)
Euromonitor projects fashion retail CAGR of 9 % (Nigeria), 7 % (Ghana), 8 % (Kenya). Assuming head-office roles scale at 60 % of sales growth, we forecast a learner pool of **≈32 000** by 2027 (compound 7 %).

## 7. Key Assumptions & Caveats
* Informal retail not counted – upside if we later certify independent boutiques.
* Head-office definition restricted to buying, merchandising, planning, VM; excludes HR/IT.
* Euromonitor employment slices extrapolated from 2022–23 share-of-wallet reports.
* LinkedIn under-representation indicates figures are conservative.

## 8. Takeaway
Even on a **20 % capture**, the Programme can enrol ~5 000 learners in three years, generating **>₦2 billion** gross revenue while addressing a visible skills gap across West & East Africa.
