---

### Insight 2: Exa's Strength = Semantic Keywords, Not Complex Logic

**Pattern:** Too many criteria = "unclear" results. Simple criteria = "match" results.

**Why this matters:**
- Exa is neural search (finds semantic matches), not boolean filter (AND/OR/NOT logic)
- Works best with 2-3 strong criteria (location, topic, follower count)
- Can't reliably verify absence ("does NOT own kitchen") from bio text alone

**Learning:** Let Exa do semantic matching (find baking instructors), let human do boolean logic (skip if owns bakery).

---

### Insight 3: Manual Filter = 10 Minutes, Not Hours

**Pattern:** User's first instinct was "more criteria = better results." Reality: more criteria = more "unclear" = wasted credits.

**Better approach:**
- Exa finds 30 accounts (2 minutes, $X credits)
- Human filters 30 bios (10 minutes, free)
- Output: 15-20 qualified leads (total time: 12 minutes)

**Lesson:** Don't over-engineer AI search. Use AI for what it's good at (semantic matching), use human for quick judgment calls (owns bakery? yes/no).

**Context:** Sourdough baking exploded during COVID, still strong in 2024-2026.
