**Why (EP M3L2):**

### 2. Keep GAS, Run Both Parallel (CRITICAL)

**Decision:** Don't quit generator sales yet. Run GAS (9-5) + Agency (evenings/weekends) in parallel.

**Why:**
- Funds low = can't afford long runway without revenue
- Agency takes 3-6 months to validate (3 clients + refine process)
- EP validation pattern: Prove it works BEFORE quitting stable income

**Exit trigger:** When agency revenue consistently > GAS revenue for 3 months

**Impact:** User will be running two businesses for 3-6 months (high energy cost, but necessary)

---

### 3. Two Different Business Models: HB vs Princess (IMPORTANT)

**Decision:** Separate HB (anchor client, ongoing retainer) from Princess (scalable offer, project-based)

**HB Studio (Anchor Client - Won't Churn):**
- Long-term relationship
- Already built: Funnel + calendar + email automation + admin dashboard (Cloudflare Workers)
- Current project: Workshop instructor lead gen (find instructors, HB does IG outreach)
- Revenue model: Retainer or project-based for ongoing work
- Workshop opportunity: ¬£750 venue booking (instructor runs 10-person workshop at ¬£75/ticket)

**Princess (Scalable Offer - Testing):**
- New client (may churn)
- Simple booking system for massage sessions
- Fixed project fee (¬£500-¬£1500 depending on tier)
- Optional maintenance retainer after delivery
- Template for future solo practitioner clients

**Why separate:** HB is custom/ongoing, Princess is repeatable/scalable. Don't try to make HB model scalable.

**Impact:** Scalable offer focus = Princess-type clients (solo practitioners needing booking systems). HB is separate revenue stream.

---

### 4. Princess Intake Process: 4-Step Async (IMPORTANT)

**Decision:** Use multimodal async intake (no 3-hour discovery calls)

**Process:**
1. **Send HB demo link:** "Play with it, book fake appointment. Voice note: what would you keep/change for massage bookings?"
2. **Voice note walkthrough:** "Walk me through ideal customer journey (how they find you ‚Üí book ‚Üí pay ‚Üí confirmation)"
3. **Screenshots/examples:** "Your current process (if any), systems you like, your branding (colors, fonts, vibe)"
4. **10-question form** (AFTER voice notes): Calendar tool, payment processor, session types, pricing, availability, cancellation policy, reminders, customer info needed, integrations

**Why:**
- Client time: 30 minutes total (not 3 calls)
- Async = no scheduling friction
- Voice notes = richer context than email
- 10 questions AFTER voice notes = informed by what they said (not generic)

**Impact:** Replicable for every client. Refines to 5 minutes after 3 clients.

**This intake process itself = part of agency offering** (professional, efficient, client-friendly)

---

### 5. Payment Timing: 50/50 Deposit (IMPORTANT)

**Decision:** 50% deposit after specs (before build), 50% on delivery

**Why:**
- Filters tire-kickers (if won't pay deposit, won't pay final)
- Covers spec/design time if client ghosts
- Professional signal (serious businesses expect deposits)
- Protects cash flow (don't work for free)

**For Princess:** ¬£250 deposit (after intake completed), ¬£250 on delivery

**Alternative considered:** Full payment upfront (harder to close), Payment on delivery only (risky if client ghosts)

**Impact:** Standard practice, reduces collections risk, professional positioning
